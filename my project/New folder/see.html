About Rwanda

Rwanda is a safe and scenic country in East Africa, famous for its rolling hills, lakes, volcanoes, and rich cultural heritage.

üèîÔ∏è Land of a Thousand Hills

Rwanda is a small but remarkably diverse country in East Africa. Known for its rolling green hills, volcanoes, lakes, and rivers, Rwanda offers breathtaking landscapes that appeal to adventurers, nature lovers, and culture enthusiasts alike.

ü¶ç Wildlife Paradise

Despite its size, the country boasts a rich variety of wildlife, including endangered mountain gorillas, golden monkeys, and over 700 bird species, making it a top destination for eco-tourism and wildlife safaris.

üèñÔ∏è Natural Beauty

Lake Kivu, one of Africa's largest freshwater lakes, provides serene beaches, water sports, and picturesque sunsets. Perfect for those seeking relaxation and scenic beauty.

üõ°Ô∏è Safety & Peace

Following the 1994 genocide, Rwanda has emerged as a symbol of peace and reconciliation. It's one of the safest African countries, with clean cities and thriving communities.

üå± Eco-Friendly Nation

Rwanda is the first plastic-bag free nation in Africa and leads in environmental conservation. Kigali is known as the cleanest city on the continent.

üé≠ Rich Culture

Rwanda is rich in culture and tradition, with welcoming people who speak Kinyarwanda, French, and English. Visitors can immerse themselves in local music, dance, art, and handicrafts.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about visiting Rwanda

Most visitors can obtain a visa on arrival at Kigali International Airport or apply for an e-visa online before travel. Citizens of African Union member states can enter Rwanda visa-free for up to 90 days.
Rwanda can be visited year-round, but the best time is during the dry seasons from June to September and December to February. These months offer ideal conditions for gorilla trekking and wildlife viewing.
A gorilla trekking permit in Rwanda costs $1,500 per person. This permit allows you one hour with a gorilla family in Volcanoes National Park.
Yes! Rwanda is consistently ranked as one of the safest countries in Africa. The country has excellent security, clean streets, and friendly people.
Rwanda has four official languages: Kinyarwanda, French, English, and Swahili. English is widely used in cities and tourist areas.
The official currency is the Rwandan Franc (RWF). US Dollars are also widely accepted in hotels and larger establishments.
Pack lightweight clothing for daytime and warmer layers for evenings. For gorilla trekking, bring sturdy hiking boots, long pants, and rain gear.
Rwanda has a well-developed transportation system. In Kigali, you can use motorcycle taxis, regular taxis, or ride-hailing apps.
