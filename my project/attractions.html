PROTECTED AREAS

Rwanda's National Parks

Five distinct parks protecting extraordinary biodiversity

üåã Volcanoes National Park

Home to endangered mountain gorillas, this misty mountain park features five volcanic peaks. Trek through bamboo forests for gorilla encounters, golden monkey tracking, and volcano hiking adventures with stunning views across three countries.

ü¶Å Akagera National Park

Rwanda's only savannah park offers Big Five safari experiences. Watch lions, elephants, rhinos, leopards, and buffalo across diverse landscapes of rolling hills, wetlands, and lakes teeming with hippos and crocodiles.

üå≤ Nyungwe Forest National Park

Ancient montane rainforest hosting 13 primate species including chimpanzees. Walk the spectacular canopy walkway 50 meters above the forest floor, trek for primates, and spot rare endemic birds in this biodiversity hotspot.

ü¶ã Gishwati-Mukura National Park

Rwanda's newest park protects restored rainforest habitat. Home to chimpanzees, golden monkeys, and over 230 bird species. A living example of successful reforestation and habitat restoration.

AFRICA'S GREAT LAKES

Lake Kivu Paradise

One of Africa's largest and most beautiful freshwater lakes, Lake Kivu offers a perfect blend of relaxation and adventure. Crystal-clear waters, sandy beaches, and stunning sunsets create an idyllic escape.

Beach Towns: Gisenyi and Kibuye feature lakeside resorts, water sports, and fresh fish restaurants. Perfect for unwinding after gorilla trekking or safari adventures.

Activities: Kayaking, stand-up paddleboarding, boat tours to islands, coffee plantation visits, and cycling along scenic shoreline roads with mountain views.

VIBRANT CAPITAL

Kigali City Experience

Africa's Cleanest City: Kigali impresses visitors with spotless streets, organized infrastructure, and thriving arts scene. Modern high-rises stand alongside traditional markets in this fast-growing capital.

Key Attractions: Kigali Genocide Memorial (powerful and educational), Caplaki Craft Village (local artisans), Inema Arts Center (contemporary African art), Kimironko Market (vibrant local life).

Dining & Nightlife: International restaurants, specialty coffee shops, rooftop bars with panoramic views, and live music venues showcase Kigali's cosmopolitan character.
