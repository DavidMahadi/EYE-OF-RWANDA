Wildlife & Conservation

Walk alongside expert rangers, track iconic species, and learn how Rwanda protects its fragile ecosystems.

Wildlife Experiences

From mountain gorillas to the Big Five, Rwanda offers world-class wildlife encounters

Mountain Gorilla Encounters

Spend a once-in-a-lifetime hour with mountain gorilla families in Volcanoes National Park, guided by experienced trackers who know every trail.

Big Five Safaris

Akagera National Park offers classic game drives with lions, elephants, rhinos, leopards, and abundant antelope species across lakes and savannah.

Birding Hotspots

Track crowned cranes, sunbirds, and forest specialists in wetlands, lakeshores, and montane forests with specialist birding guides.

Behind-the-Scenes Conservation

Visit ranger posts, tree nurseries, and community projects to understand how Rwanda balances tourism with long-term protection.

Golden Monkey Trekking

Track playful golden monkeys through bamboo forests in Volcanoes National Park, a unique primate experience.

Chimpanzee Tracking

Nyungwe Forest National Park offers chimpanzee tracking experiences through ancient rainforest canopies.

Conservation Success Stories

Rwanda's commitment to protecting wildlife has led to remarkable recoveries

Gorilla Population Growth

Mountain gorilla numbers have increased significantly thanks to dedicated conservation efforts and community involvement.

Lion Reintroduction

Akagera National Park successfully reintroduced lions after their local extinction, now thriving in protected savannah.

Rhino Protection

Black rhinos have been reintroduced to Akagera with 24/7 protection from dedicated ranger teams.

Forest Restoration

Community-led reforestation projects have restored critical habitats for primates and bird species.
