ONCE IN A LIFETIME

Mountain Gorilla Encounters

Trek through misty bamboo forests in the shadow of volcanic peaks to spend an unforgettable hour with mountain gorilla families. Expert trackers who've known these gorillas for years lead you through their territory, sharing insights about individual personalities and family dynamics.

With only 1,000 mountain gorillas left in the wild, this intimate encounter is both a privilege and a powerful conservation experience. Your permit directly funds protection efforts and community programs.

Permits are $1,500 per person and must be booked months in advance. The experience includes a briefing on gorilla etiquette, trekking through Volcanoes National Park, and one magical hour observing these gentle giants in their natural habitat.

PRIMATE PARADISE

Beyond Gorillas

Golden Monkey Trekking: These playful, endangered primates inhabit bamboo forests in Volcanoes National Park. Smaller groups and lower permit costs ($100) make this an accessible primate experience.

Chimpanzee Tracking: Nyungwe Forest National Park hosts over 400 chimpanzees. Trek through ancient rainforest listening for their distinctive calls, then watch these intelligent apes feed, play, and interact.

13 Primate Species: Rwanda protects an incredible diversity of primates including colobus monkeys, L'Hoest's monkeys, and numerous smaller species found nowhere else.

NYUNGWE FOREST

Ancient Rainforest Sanctuary

One of Africa's oldest rainforests, Nyungwe shelters 13 primate species, 275 bird species, and over 1,000 plant species. The forest plays a crucial role in East Africa's water cycle, feeding rivers that flow to the Nile and Congo basins.

Walk the canopy walkway suspended 50 meters above the forest floor for a bird's-eye view of this pristine ecosystem. Morning mist, howling monkeys, and the calls of hornbills create an atmosphere unlike anywhere else.
