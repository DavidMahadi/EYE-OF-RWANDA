Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about visiting Rwanda

Most visitors can obtain a visa on arrival at Kigali International Airport or apply for an e-visa online before travel. Citizens of African Union member states can enter Rwanda visa-free for up to 90 days. Check the latest requirements before your trip.
Rwanda can be visited year-round, but the best time is during the dry seasons from June to September and December to February. These months offer ideal conditions for gorilla trekking and wildlife viewing with less rainfall and clearer skies.
A gorilla trekking permit in Rwanda costs $1,500 per person. This permit allows you one hour with a gorilla family in Volcanoes National Park. Permits should be booked well in advance as they sell out quickly, especially during peak season.
Yes! Rwanda is consistently ranked as one of the safest countries in Africa. The country has excellent security, clean streets, and friendly people. Crime rates are very low, and tourists are welcomed warmly throughout the country.
Rwanda has four official languages: Kinyarwanda, French, English, and Swahili. English is widely used in cities and tourist areas, making it easy for English-speaking visitors to communicate.
The official currency is the Rwandan Franc (RWF). US Dollars are also widely accepted in hotels and larger establishments. Credit cards are accepted in major hotels and restaurants, and ATMs are available in cities.
Pack lightweight clothing for daytime and warmer layers for evenings. For gorilla trekking, bring sturdy hiking boots, long pants, rain gear, and gloves. Don't forget sunscreen, insect repellent, and a good camera for wildlife photography.
Rwanda has a well-developed transportation system. In Kigali, you can use motorcycle taxis, regular taxis, or ride-hailing apps. For longer distances, private vehicles with drivers or domestic flights are recommended. We can arrange all transportation as part of your itinerary.
A yellow fever vaccination certificate is required for entry into Rwanda. It's also recommended to be up-to-date on routine vaccinations and consider hepatitis A, typhoid, and malaria prophylaxis. Consult your doctor before travel.
Absolutely! Rwanda is very family-friendly. While gorilla trekking has age restrictions (typically 15+), there are many activities suitable for children including cultural villages, gentle nature walks, boat rides, and interactive museums.
